"Linh, please recommend me a good book." — an ask I never quite know how to answer. 📚

Honestly, this question always makes me a little shy, also kind of proud? 🥹 It means you see me as a reader, and you trust me enough to ask. So let me try my best.

The thing is, recommending books is hard. Books - like music, like films, like most things that matter - are deeply personal. What moved me might leave you cold. What I couldn't put down might be exactly the wrong book for where you are right now. 😅

Fair warning though - most of these books are not plot-heavy. If you're looking for something gripping and fast-paced, you might be a little disappointed. But if you're in the mood to sit with some big human feelings, get a little calmer, think a little differently, or just find the words for something you've been quietly carrying - I think you'll find something here. 🌿

There's a mix of everything on these shelves - from classics that have been around for decades like 1984 and The Little Prince, to recent bestsellers you might have spotted at Kinokuniya or seen floating around BookTok. Old or new, they all earned their place here.

I came across a scene in Welcome to the Hyunam-dong Bookshop where the bookshop owner finds herself in the same dilemma - she realises she's been pushing her own favourites onto customers without really listening first, and it doesn't go well. So she makes herself a rule:

Be objective. Don't rush. Ask what they've been reading, what's been on their mind, what they're in the mood for.

I thought that was smart. So instead of just dumping my favourites on you, I grouped them by how you might be feeling right now. 🫶 Pick the shelf that sounds most like you today.

There are more than 20 books here - mostly quiet, character-driven stories, a lot of East Asian authors, a few non-fiction thrown in. Some you can finish in an afternoon, some will take a week. All of them meant something to me, and I hope you find one that does the same. 🤍

And regardless of whether you're picking up a book for the first time in years, trying to get back into reading, or just here because I wouldn't stop talking about books - I'm really glad we now share this. 🫰

- Linh
